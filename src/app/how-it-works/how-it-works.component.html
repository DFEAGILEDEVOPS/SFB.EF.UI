How the Efficiency Metric Ranking works

The efficiency metric is an overview of how well your school uses its funding to improve pupil progress, compared to similar schools.

What you will see

You will see 50 schools including your school which are ranked by their Efficiency Metric Rank and divided into deciles, 10 groups of 5. If a school is in the first decile it is among the five most efficient schools and if a school is in the tenth decile it is among the five least efficient schools.

Methodology

The efficiency metric algorithm looks for 50 schools across the country which are similar to your school based on:

  • Number of pupils
  • Pupils eligible for FSM in the last 6 years
  • Pupils with a SEN statement or EHCP
  • School phase
  • School type

We divide these school's progress score by their expenditure per pupil (we will standardise the score) allowing us to rank the 50 schools.

Standardised progress score / Expenditure per pupil = Efficiency Metric Ranking

What to do with this information

We understand that lots of different issues can affect pupil progress. To investigate this properly, you may need to use other tools and talk to your management team. On the efficiency metric page we have listed some tools we think would be of use.

We have also included the contact details of all the listed schools to allow you to see what knowledge you could share and if you could collaborate.
